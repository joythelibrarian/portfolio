About

I'm an academic librarian and educator committed to inclusive, student-centered instruction. My work bridges information literacy, multilingual learner support, and critical research skills across community college, university, and K–12 contexts.

This portfolio brings together instructional slide decks, video tutorials, LibGuides, curriculum materials, and eLearning projects from Berkeley City College, Portland State University, Portland Community College, and related teaching engagements.

Course-integrated library instruction designed for Berkeley City College students across disciplines — including composition, business, health, history, social work, and design. Sessions emphasize source evaluation, database strategy, and research as an iterative process.

Workshop · Library Orientation

BCC Library Role-Playing Game Workshop — "Troll-free Berkeley"

Led a Flex Day workshop in Spring 2018 as an interactive library orientation. Participants installed a mobile role-playing game (built on the ARIS platform) onto their devices and played through a quest that taught students how to find books using the Library of Congress classification system and navigate the Berkeley City College campus building.

Kim, J. (2018, Spring). Troll-free Berkeley: A library orientation role-playing game [BCC Library Flex Day Workshop]. Berkeley City College Library.
